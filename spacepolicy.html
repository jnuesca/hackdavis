National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958

July 29, 1958

The National Aeronautics and Space Act enacted during the Eisenhower presidency created the administration bearing the same name in response to incapability of solely the United States military to keep up with the space race against the Soviets. The newly formed agency aimed to preserve the role of the United States as a leader in aeronautical and space science and technology, to develop and operate vehicles capable of carrying instruments, equipment, supplies and living organisms through space, and to expand human knowledge of phenomena in the atmosphere and space.
