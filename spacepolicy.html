The History of United States Space Policy

1950s-present

The Beginning of the Space Race

Sputnik

October 4, 1957

This was the launch of the world's first artificial satellite into orbit by the Soviet Union. The satellite fell out of orbit and burned up in the atmosphere the next year, but marked the beginning of the space race.

National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958

July 29, 1958

This act created the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), and outlined their purpose. NASA was tasked with expanding human knowledge. They were also strictly limited to peaceful operations, so that space would not become the battlefield for World War III. However, this Act was an escalation of the arms race, which had built up after WWII between the United States and the Soviet Union. Read more here
